HTML Semantic Tags

  1. <header> : Represents introductory content, typically at the beginning of a section or a page.

    <header>
    <h1>Website Name</h1>   
    <nav>
        <ul>
            <li><a href="#home">Home</a></li>
            <li><a href="#about">About</a></li>
            <li><a href="#contact">Contact</a></li>
        </ul>
    </nav>
</header>

  2. <nav> : Defines navigation links within the document.

    <nav>
    <ul>
        <li><a href="#home">Home</a></li>
        <li><a href="#about">About</a></li>
        <li><a href="#contact">Contact</a></li>
    </ul>
</nav>

  3. <main> : Represents the main content of the document, excluding header, footer, and sidebar content.

    <main>
    <article>
        <h2>Article Title</h2>
        <p>This is the content of the article.</p>
    </article>
    <aside>
        <h3>Related Articles</h3>
        <ul>
            <li><a href="#related1">Related Article 1</a></li>
            <li><a href="#related2">Related Article 2</a></li>
            <li><a href="#related3">Related Article 3</a></li>
        </ul>
    </aside>
</main>

  4. <section> : Represents a thematic grouping of content, typically with a heading.

    <section>
    <h2>Section Title</h2>
    <p>This is the content of the section.</p>
</section>

  5. <article> : Represents self-contained content that can be independently distributed and reused.

    <article>
    <h2>Article Title</h2>
    <p>This is the content of the article.</p>
</article>

  6. <aside> : Defines content that is tangentially related to the content around it, such as sidebars or pull quotes.

    <aside>
    <h2>Related Content Title</h2>
    <p>This is the related content.</p>
</aside>

  7. <footer> : Represents footer content, typically at the end of a section or a page.

    <footer>
    <p>&copy; 2024 Example Company. All rights reserved.</p>
</footer>

  8. <figure> : Represents self-contained content, such as images or diagrams, with optional caption.

    <figure>
    <img src="example.jpg alt="Example Image">
    <figcaption>This is the caption for the image.</figcaption>
</figure>

  9. <figcaption> : Represents the caption or legend for a <figure> element.

    <figure>
    <img src="example.jpg alt="Example Image">
    <figcaption>This is the caption for the image.</figcaption>
</figure>

  10. <details> : Defines additional details or information that can be toggled open or closed.

    <details>
    <summary>Click to expand</summary>
    <p>Additional details here...</p>
</details>
